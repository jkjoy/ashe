There is a practical difference between having time and having shape. A free morning can still disappear quickly if it opens without any rhythm at all.

I prefer a small sequence: open the window, make tea, write one page by hand, and only then start reading or replying. It is less about productivity than about pacing.

That order gives the day a tone. The work still changes, but the first hour no longer feels accidental.
